The Marine Pollution Prevention Authority (MPPA) was established in 1991 inaccordance with the Marine Pollution Prevention (MPP) Act No 59 of 1981.Initially, it operated under the Ministry of Port and Shipping until 1994. However,due to increasing challenges in marine pollution prevention in Sri Lanka as a resultof global advancements and economic development activities, the act was repealed.

In its place, the Marine Pollution Prevention Act No. 35 of 2008 was introduced andbecame effective on January 1, 2009. This new legislation took into considerationthe changing circumstances and aimed to address marine pollution more effectively.As part of this reform, the Authority's name was changed to the MarineEnvironment Protection Authority (MEPA).

Presently, the Marine Environment Protection Authority operates under the purviewof the Minister of Urban Development & housing. This act has strengthened the lawenforcement capabilities of the Authority, allowing them to tackle any acts ofmarine environment pollution more efficiently and effectively.
